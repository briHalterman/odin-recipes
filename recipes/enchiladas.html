Enchiladas

Description

Baked enchiladas are a comforting, flavor-packed dish made by rolling tortillas around a savory filling—typically shredded chicken, beef, beans, or cheese—then smothering them in a rich, seasoned sauce and melted cheese. Once assembled, the enchiladas are baked until the cheese is bubbly and golden and the flavors meld together beautifully. Whether topped with a classic red enchilada sauce or a creamy verde, they're often served with rice, beans, sour cream, and fresh garnishes like cilantro or avocado, making them a satisfying and crowd-pleasing meal.

Ingredients

  • Tortillas
  • Ground Beef
  • Taco Seasoning
  • Enchilada Sauce
  • Shredded Cheese

Steps

  1. Brown ground beef with taco seasoning.
  2. Roll tortillas with some meat, cheese and sauce and place them in a baking dish.
  3. Cover with remaining sauce and then top with cheese.
  4. Bake at 350° until hot all the way through and cheese is golden brown.
